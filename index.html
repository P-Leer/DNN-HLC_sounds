Hearing Loss Compensation Audio Examples

System Explanations

Below are several hearing loss compensation systems with corresponding audio examples across different signal-to-noise ratios (SNRs). Here’s a brief explanation of each system:

  • Unprocessed: The original noisy audio without any processing.
  • HLC (audiogram divided by 2): This system is derived from the audiogram divided by 2 in the log domain of the auditory model.
  • HLC (audiogram without division) : This system uses the full audiogram without division in the log domain.
  • NR_NAL_R: This system applies Noise Reduction followed by NAL-R (National Acoustic Laboratories—Revised) filtering.
  • HLC-NR (audiogram divided by 2): This system applies the audiogram divided by 2 in the log domain along with Noise Reduction.
  • DNN-HA: Deep Neural Network Hearing Aid system. More details available at DNN-HA GitHub.
  • HLC (IC, audiogram divided by 2): Similar to the audiogram divided by 2 system, but appended with Inferior Colliculus (IC) modeling.
  • HLC (IC, audiogram not divided by 2): Similar to the no division, but appended with Inferior Colliculus (IC) modeling.
  • HLC-NR (IC, audiogram divided by 2): Noise Reduction system, auditory nerve model appended with Inferior Colliculus (IC) modeling. Uses Wave-U-Net (WUN).
